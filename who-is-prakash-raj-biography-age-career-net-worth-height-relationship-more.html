Who Is Prakash Raj? Biography, Age, Career, Net Worth, Height, Relationship &amp; More

Prakash Raj is an Indian actor, film director, producer, and TV presenter who primarily works in the South Indian film industry. Prakash was introduced to Indian film director K Balachander by one of his co-stars in the Kannada film Harakeya Kuri (1993).

Prakash Raj is an actor and producer, known for Anniyan (2005), Abhiyum Naanum (2008), and Kalki (1996). On the advice of renowned Tamil director K Balachander, actor Prakash Rai changed his name to Prakash Raj. The director believed that Prakash had the skills to become an all-Indian actor, so he asked Prakash to keep the Raj suffix.

Prakash Raj’s Biography

Prakash Raj born Prakash Rai on 26 March 1965, is an Indian actor, film director, producer, television host, and politician. Known for his films in Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and Hindi, he has won 5 National Film Awards, 8 Nandi Awards, 8 Tamil Nadu Film Awards, 5 Filmfare Awards South, and won multiple awards including the SIIMA Award and three Cinema Awards.

Besides his native Kannada, Prakash Raj is fluent in Telugu, Tamil, Malayalam, Hindi, English, and Marathi, making him one of the most in-demand actors in Indian cinema. After years of working in stage shows and television in Kannada, Prakash Raj ventured into film. He made his debut in Tamil cinema with K. Balachander’s Duet (1994) and has since become a commercially successful Tamil film star. In his memory, he named his production company Duet Movies.

As a multilingual, he has played a variety of roles, mostly as an antagonist and more recently as a character actor. As an actor, Prakash Raj won Best Supporting Actor for Mani Ratnam`s Iruvar in Ratnam in 1998 and Best Supporting Actor in the Telugu film Antahpuram directed by Krishnavamsi in 1998. In 2007, he was recognized for his role in the Priyadarshan-directed Tamil film, Kanchivaram.

As a producer, Prakash Raj won the 2011 National Film Award for Best Feature Film in Kannada for ‘Puttakkan Highway’ directed by his longtime theater friend B. Suresha. Prakash also hosted Neengalum Vellalam Oru Kodi during the second season of the series. Prakash Raj came to Kalakshetra, Bengaluru in the early stages of his career, appearing in a series of stage shows for 300 rupees a month and giving 2,000 street theater performances.

Prakash Raj took over directing and co-producing Naanu Nanna Kanasu in 2010’s Kannada. The film ran for 125 days in cinemas in Karnataka and became the biggest hit of the year. He was nominated for Filmfare’s South Best Director category for his first film.

He then directed the Telugu and Tamil bilingual film Dhoni. In 2014, he directed the multilingual films Oggarane (Kannada), Ulavacharu Biryani (Telugu), and Un Samayal Arayil (Tamil). While the Tamil and Telugu versions did not perform miracles at the box office, the Kannada version was the blockbuster of the year.

Prakash Raj became a film producer from the Tamil film Daya (2002) starring actress Meena. He won the Special Jury National Award for his performance. He later produced Tamil films such as Naam (2003), Azhagiya Theeye (2004), Kanda Naal Mudhal (2005), Poi (2006), Mozhi (2007), Velli Thirai, Abhiyum Naanum, and in 2008 for Many Tamil he won Nadu State Award.

Prakash Raj adopted the villages of Kondareddypalle, Mahabubnagar district, Telangana state, and Bandlarahatti village, Chitradurga district, Karnataka state. He started an aggressive political campaign using the hashtag #justasking on social media after the assassination of his friend Gauri Lankesh in September 2017.

Prakash Raj ran as an independent candidate for the Bengaluru Central Lok Sabha constituency in the 2019 Indian general election but he lost the election, receiving approximately 28,906 votes (2.41%) in the election.

Prakash Raj’s Career

Prakash Raj began his acting career with television serials like Bisilu Kudure (Kannada) and Guddada Bhootha (Tulu and Kannada). After beginning his career in Kannada television shows, the actor proved his mettle in his first movie, the Tamil film, Duet, in 1994.

Prakash Raj has also established himself as a firebrand politician. He never minces words when it comes to speaking his mind and as a producer has produced a lot of films in the Indian movie industry.
