Aaron Gordon | Charity

The NBA player has been associated with GF2 ( Gordon Family Foundation) which was founded in 2018 by Gordon and his family.

The foundation works to incubate and mentor young unrepresented students. Meanwhile, their activities include food distribution, basketball workshops, donations, etc. 

As a part of the foundation, Gordon donated to the Homeless Education Fund in Orange County during the pandemic in 2020. 

Moreover, Gordon co-won the 2019 Rich and Helen DeVos Community Enrichment Award for his contribution towards helping the community’s children.

As part of the award-winning, the player was provided an additional $25,000 to donate to his charity of choice.

Aaron Gordon | Career

Aaron’s career started early when he was just in high school. He started from his school varsity team, where he played for four years.

Consequently, he was chosen as Mr. Calfornia Basketball Player of the Year when he was just a junior at high school. 

After that, the dunk star chose to join the University of Arizona. Just after playing a year of college basketball, the player decided to go for the NBA Draft. He was picked by the Orlando Magic during the 2014 NBA Draft. 

Gordon played with the Orlando Magic till 2020 with a renewal in 2018. Later in 2021, the player came in agreement with the Denver Nuggets to join them as their forward.
